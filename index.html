Summary

Tejas is a mechanical engineering graduate with a master's degree in aerospace engineering, specializing in propulsion. His work is primarily focused on numerical modeling of fluid mechanics and combustion, with a strong interest in connecting simulations to physical reality.

He works extensively with CFD across RANS and LES, with exposure to DNS, and places particular emphasis on verification, validation, and model fidelity rather than black-box simulation. Alongside this, he has hands-on experimental experience in laboratory-scale combustion and flow diagnostics, giving him a practical understanding of measurement uncertainty and data quality.

Comfortable with Python, C++, and FORTRAN, he develops efficient post-processing and HPC workflows, and is currently exploring machine-learning-assisted data analysis and CFD to augment traditional numerical approaches.
